What is Stock??

Stock is security that represents the ownership of a fraction of a corporation. The term "security" refers to a fungible, negotiable financial instrument that holds some type of monetary value. This entitles the owner of the stock to a proportion of the corporation's assets and profits equal to how much stock they own. Units of stock are called "shares." Investors can buy and sell, privately or publicly, corporate assets predominately through a stock exchange system while conforming to government regulations. These regulations are to protect investors from fraudulent practices.

When corporations issue stocks they are raising funds to operate their businesses. When an investor who owns a stock, the shareholder owns a portion of the company. If the the shareholder owns 100 shares out of the 1,000 shares of stock outstanding, they own and claim 10% of the company's assets and earnings. Shareholders do not technically own corporations; they own shares issued by corporations. Note that corporations are treated as legal persons in the sense they can file taxes, borrow, own property, and be sued. The distinction is important because the corporation are the true owners of their own assets. Shareholders are simply making money when the company makes money through their investments by recieving dividends.
